Give+Seek | About

Give, with an open heart.

Seek, with no hesitation.

Give+Seek is a place where the community comes together in times of need. People have the opportunity to provide unconditionally to those who need assistance, and those in need can ask without hesitation from those who are kind.

The project was created during January 26-27, 2019 for nwHacks 2019.

Watch a video of the presentation and mockup demo.

The Inspiration

The recent government shutdown from December 22, 2018 to January 25, 2019 in the US brought hardships on nearly a million government employees, who went unpaid for a record span of 35 days. Many were in need of food, daycare, and shelter after a single missed paycheck, relying on food banks and donors. Local and federal resources were drained to support the masses of unpaid workers, and they were encouraged to take on additional jobs.

"Never in a million years would I have ever dreamed that I’d be turning to GoFundMe asking for help, because in all honesty, I’ve always felt there was probably someone else who could use the help more than my family."

Jo Ann Goodlow, GoFundMe: Government Shutdown

On a more sombre note, during the attacks on the United States of America on September 11, 2001, over 4,000 planes were grounded all over North America and unable to fly. Over 200 planes were diverted to Canada, 34 of which landed in Vancouver.

From the areas where thousands of people waited, terrified and alone, communities reached out with warm hands to support and help wherever they could. Gander, Newfoundland provided the warmest welcome; the town of only 10,000 provided medicine, water, food, and shelter to a stranded group of 10,000 more. Schools, shelters, local buildings, and any feasible spaces were repurposed for temporary shelter. A pregnant woman was well taken care of by medical professionals and nurses. Doctors and high school students alike sprang to help any way they could.

"At a time when the world, for many, seemed to be falling apart, many Canadians, in Gander, Halifax and many other places, came together to open their houses, stores and hearts to perfect strangers. And I dare to predict that neither the Canadians nor the strangers will ever forget the experience."

Bruce Ricketts, 9/11 - The Day the Planes Stood Still

The hospitality, compassion, and welcoming love of the town of Gander for those who needed help was incomparable. Our question was this: How can we provide people with ways to continue helping those in need in our community?

Target Consumers

We analyzed those who are most vulnerable in society - those who rely on social support and whatever jobs they can find. These people may be isolated from those who are not struggling as often, and form groups amongst each other. Given how they are barely able to make enough to live within their means, once in a while they likely find themselves in hard situations with nobody to help.

We juxtaposed this group of people with those who are more comfortable with their means of life, but still live in the same community. They are likely still less affluent than the average citizen, which gives them the ability to empathize closely with those who undergo temporary hardships. They are afforded the ability to stock up on additional supplies and household items when discounted in stores, and will keep these at home until they are of use.

Solution

Our solution is a web application platform which connects the community in times of need. Those who are able and willing to provide for and support those around them can list items that they have. Those in need can bypass social stigma to ask the community for specific assistance based on their urgent need.

Whenever someone offers an item which is sought by another person, they are matched with the recipient. After accepting, the giver and seeker connect to exchange the item.

User Experience Design

To create the best resultant experience of users, we delved into their needs, perceptions, and ideal interactions.

We began by referencing past and current situations such as the people who were affected by government shutdown discussed earlier, and occurrences we would expect to see in everyday life such as international students working multiple jobs to support their tuition and housing. Using this, we built a conceptualization of what types of items and services would be required, as well as how they would naturally and intuitively interact with the application to fulfill their needs.

User Experience Prototype

To visually understand how users would navigate and use the web application, we designed a prototype using Adobe XD. This prototype details the user experience through a set of steps arranged sequentially.

The initial screen is a style tile created to illustrate the visual design language, color scheme, and font choice for the application as a whole.

First, the user is shown a graphic which illustrates the fundamental idea of Giving and Seeking. They scroll down through a set of short descriptions to orient them to the purpose of the site, and choose whether to give or seek. The user is taken through a short process to register, then are provided with sample categories of items they can provide or ask for.

At the main screen, users can input their items and services and choose from provided autofill options predicted from the input fields. When another user asks for the same item which was provided, a notification is received by the giver who can connect with the seeker and meet up to provide the item. Afterwards, the seeker marks the request as fulfilled and the process is complete.

Navigate through the user experience prototype yourself, hosted on Adobe's website. Blue indicators show the next target to click to advance the prototype.

Technology Stack

Web Application (Angular & Bootstrap)

The front end of the web application is built using Angular, to create the single-page application with styling and scripting, and Bootstrap to provide styling support. These provided a strong and flexible platform on which to build the application.

The Home, About, Give Submission, Seek Submission, Give Notification, Seek Notification, Header, and Footer are all individual components assembled to create the final product.

Server (Node.js & Express)

The back end server was written in Node.js with the Express framework. It provides data storage, processing, and communication in tandem with the front end component.

Infrastructure (Google Cloud Platform & Google App Engine)

To host the server, Google Cloud Platform was chosen.

A Google App Engine instance was utilized to manage the server temporarily for the hackathon but is no longer active. The server is now hosted on Heroku at https://giveandseek.herokuapp.com/.

As an unfortunate lesson, we learned that Google App Engine may take approximately 10 minutes to rebuild and redeploy changes due to the requirement of rebuilding multiple layers of an image and transferring the entire image to the server. This is in comparison to a provider like Heroku, which is able to autodeploy upon changes to a remote repository resulting in the server updated and restarted within a minute.

Positioning

In relation to Facebook's Crisis Response, this does not attempt to replace social media networks. In addition, Give+Seek does not aim to spread knowledge in the event of an emergency, only provide support for those caught in the situation. A path of future development could be to provide easier access and event-based support, relevant to real-time situations.

In relation to Craigslist, this does not act as a marketplace even though items are exchanged. Those who use the application have no encouragement at any time to seek monetary repayment for their generosity. A path of future development could be to provide redeemable credits for givers on the service and reward them through unique non-monetary means.

Objective

The potential of human connection in times of need cannot be understated. During tragedies or emergencies we mobilized when given the opportunity to help others, but every day people's lives and well-being are still at stake. We can still be there to help them, and to provide the community with ways to help each other - and that is the key objective of Give+Seek.

Those who have need may come to seek for what they need, when they need it most. They seek for kindness from those who have compassion to spare. Those who have resources to help others may give back to the community that cares for them.

We are here for each other - as a community.
